Overview

The scope of this project was to redesign the company website for Interware Development, Inc., a software firm that specializes in payment processing for government and academia. The redesign was to be performed with the following goals in mind:

  • Provide visitors with a more engaging experience that is optimized for multiple device screen sizes

  • Modernize the design to attract new customers and retain existing ones, achieving increased conversions via the “Contact Us” form

  • Expand upon the amount of information currently available to visitors

Research

Research on how to design the new site was conducted by asking the client a series of questions, of which the following are some examples:

  • What are the business goals for the redesign? Are there any measurable indicators of success using analytics?

  • How do you want to represent your brand online? What is the company's personality and how should that translate into Look & Feel?

  • What are the products / services / company values that you want to make customers most aware of?

  • What groups do the site’s users fall into and what are each of them looking for?

  • Based on a provided list of existing model sites, what do you like/dislike about each of them?

  • Are there internal technology platforms that you'd like to integrate with the site currently or in the future? Which browsers need to be supported?

Wireframes

Summary

Wireframes were created in Balsamiq and presented to the client prior to give a picture of the overall planned site layout. Adjustments were made iteratively based on feedback, and approval obtained prior to moving on to implementation.
